# Ophanim Loot Pull Tables

Draft target: six level-3 PCs.

Current design direction:

- The Ophanim fight should not require tracking a pile of bespoke loot effects.
- Each wing has only two active abilities:
  - one normal attack
  - one stronger thematic attack/effect that normally costs Fear
- Bad loot rolls immediately trigger the associated wing instead of creating long-duration penalties.
- Good loot rolls mostly create one-use access tokens with simple attack riders.
- The Eye-Cluster Crown remains immune and untargetable until all four wings are Destroyed, except for Diamond Access.

## Core Pull Procedure

The central loot-box torso has four levers:

- Money / Wing of Privilege
- Stress / Wing of Scarcity
- Hope / Wing of Vanity
- Hit Points / Wing of Debt

Pulling a lever is an action unless a result says otherwise.

Costs:

- Money Pull: spend a meaningful amount of coin, treasure, ammunition, supply, or valuables. The exact amount can be abstracted by the GM.
- Stress Pull: mark 1 Stress.
- Hope Pull: spend 1 Hope.
- Hit Point Pull: mark 1 HP. This cost cannot be reduced by armor or resistance.

Roll 1d10 on the matching table.

## Divine Paywall

The Ophanim's wings are immune to ordinary damage unless an encounter loot result says the wing can be damaged.

Avoid Daggerheart condition keywords for this encounter's loot access. Say "the wing can be damaged" or "the attack can damage that wing."

The Eye-Cluster Crown is different:

- It is immune, untargetable, and unreachable until all four wings are Destroyed.
- Once all four wings are Destroyed, the Ophanim crashes and the eye becomes targetable.
- **Diamond Access** is the only loot that can damage the eye before all four wings are Destroyed. If used before the crash, the attack can mark at most 1 HP on the eye regardless of damage.

## Access Tokens

Access tokens are the main loot object. A PC can hold only one access token at a time. If they gain another, they choose which one to keep.

Spend an access token before making an attack. The token is spent after the attack resolves, hit or miss.

Access tiers:

- **Discount Access:** Your next attack can damage the associated wing, but the token carries the listed drawback.
- **Basic Access:** Your next attack can damage the associated wing.
- **Bronze Access:** Your next attack can damage the associated wing and gains the listed result-6 bonus.
- **Silver Access:** Your next attack can damage the associated wing and gains the listed result-7 bonus.
- **Gold Access:** Your next attack can damage the associated wing and gains the listed result-8 bonus.
- **Platinum Access:** Your next attack can damage any wing, but not the Eye-Cluster Crown.
- **Diamond Access:** Your next attack can damage any wing or the Eye-Cluster Crown.

If an access token says the attack marks extra HP, that only applies if the attack already marks at least 1 HP.

## Bad Roll Triggers

Bad rolls do not create ongoing encounter effects.

| d10 | Result Pattern |
| --- | --- |
| 1 | The associated wing immediately uses its Fear attack against the pulling PC. This does not cost the GM Fear. |
| 2 | The associated wing immediately uses its normal attack against the pulling PC. |
| 3 | The pulling PC loses 1 of the associated resource. |
| 4 | The pulling PC gains Discount Access against the associated wing. |
| 5 | The pulling PC gains Basic Access against the associated wing and suffers a useless cosmetic change. |
| 6-8 | The pulling PC gains Bronze, Silver, or Gold Access against the associated wing plus stronger bonuses. Result 8 should include multiple benefits. |
| 9 | The pulling PC gains Platinum Access plus immediate bonuses. |
| 10 | The pulling PC gains Diamond Access plus immediate jackpot bonuses. |

## Wing Attacks

These are draft attack models. Tune attack modifiers and damage to match the final segment stat blocks.

### Wing of Privilege

- **Normal Attack: Golden Repeater.** Far range, attack +2, 2d6+4 magic damage. A clean, expensive shot from jeweled feathers.
- **Fear Attack: Premium Enforcement.** Spend 1 Fear, Far range, attack +3, 2d8+4 magic damage. On a hit, the target also loses a meaningful item, supply, ammunition cache, or treasure. If they cannot lose something appropriate, they mark 1 Stress instead.

### Wing of Scarcity

- **Normal Attack: Countdown Needles.** Far range, attack +2, 2d6+3 magic damage. Red clock-hand feathers spear toward the target.
- **Fear Attack: Hurry Hurry Hurry.** Spend 1 Fear, Far range, attack +3, 2d8+3 magic damage. On a hit, the target also marks 1 Stress, and their next action roll made to attack, wait, study, aim carefully, or resist pressure has disadvantage.

### Wing of Vanity

- **Normal Attack: Mirror Lash.** Very Close range, attack +2, 2d6+3 magic damage. A mirrored feather cuts with the target's own perfected silhouette.
- **Fear Attack: The Better You.** Spend 1 Fear, Far range, attack +3, 2d8+3 magic damage. On a hit, the target loses 1 Hope. If they have no Hope, they mark 1 Stress instead.

### Wing of Debt

- **Normal Attack: Ledger Chain.** Close range, attack +2, 2d6+4 physical damage. Barbed creditor chains pull the target toward the body.
- **Fear Attack: Repossession Miracle.** Spend 1 Fear, Close range, attack +3, 2d8+4 physical damage. On a hit, the target marks 1 HP in addition to any HP marked by the damage. This added HP cannot be reduced by armor or resistance.

### Eye-Cluster Crown

The Eye-Cluster Crown does not act until it can be targeted, unless the GM wants it to speak, judge, or telegraph danger.

- **Normal Attack: Many-Eyed Judgment.** Far range, attack +3, 2d8+4 magic damage.
- **Fear Attack: Terms Updated.** Spend 1 Fear, Far range, attack +4, 2d10+4 magic damage. On a hit, the target also loses 1 Hope or marks 1 Stress, GM's choice.

## Optional Pity Valve

Use this only if the table is rolling cold and the encounter starts to stall.

Pity starts at 0. Increase Pity by 1 whenever a pull rolls 1-3.

At Pity 3, the next pull gains +2 to the d10 result, to a maximum of 10, then Pity resets to 0.

Do not use Pity if the party is already regularly getting access tokens and damaging wings.

## Segment Protection Summary

Draft wing protections:

- Wing of Privilege: cannot be damaged unless an access token or Money-table result says it can be damaged.
- Wing of Scarcity: cannot be damaged unless an access token or Stress-table result says it can be damaged.
- Wing of Vanity: cannot be damaged unless an access token or Hope-table result says it can be damaged.
- Wing of Debt: cannot be damaged unless an access token or Hit Point-table result says it can be damaged.
- Eye-Cluster Crown: immune and untargetable until all four wings are Destroyed, except when a PC spends Diamond Access.

## Tuning Notes

If the fight stalls:

- Turn on the optional Pity valve.
- Let result 5 attacks add +1d6 damage.
- Let Platinum Access from result 9 damage the eye after all wings are Destroyed.

If the fight is too easy:

- Diamond Access cannot target the eye until at least two wings are Destroyed.
- Result 8 loses either advantage or the +1d6 damage bonus.
- Increase each wing by 1 HP.

If the tables feel too punitive:

- Result 1 costs the GM 1 Fear instead of being free.
- Result 3 drains the associated resource only if the player has any; otherwise nothing happens.
- Discount Access on result 4 loses its drawback after the first wing is Destroyed.

If the tables feel too generous:

- Access tokens expire if not spent before the holder's next pull.
- Result 10 Diamond Access cannot mark bonus HP against the Eye-Cluster Crown.
- Result 9 Platinum Access can only damage any wing once at least one wing has been Destroyed.
