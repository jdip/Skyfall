# The Glocktopus

Working names:

- The Glocktopus
- The Eight-Armed Arsenal
- The Many-Caliber Leviathan
- The Kraken of the Last Magazine
- Saint Calamari of the Second Amendment

Core line:

> It learned that fear sounds different through every barrel.

## Concept

A colossal octopus that has adapted to land, gunpowder, and humanoid panic. Each tentacle carries, grows around, or has fused with a different firearm. It is not a disciplined soldier; it is a living weapons platform with the wet intelligence of a deep-sea predator and the showy confidence of someone who brought eight guns to an argument.

The Glocktopus should feel funny at first glance and terrifying by the second round. The joke is the silhouette: giant octopus, too many guns. The horror is the coordination: every tentacle solves a different tactical problem, and the party must decide which guns to disable before the battlefield becomes impossible.

## Foundry Structure Target

Existing colossi in the campaign use a framework actor plus segment actors. This colossus should follow that pattern:

- Framework: The Glocktopus
- Segment: Revolver Tentacle
- Segment: Shotgun Tentacle
- Segment: Rifle Tentacle
- Segment: Gatling Tentacle
- Segment: Cannon Tentacle
- Segment: Harpoon Tentacle
- Segment: Flare Mortar Tentacle
- Segment: Net Launcher Tentacle
- Final weak point: Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage

The tentacles are the phase-one objectives. The mantle is the final target, but it begins protected by crossed tentacles, ink, and suppressive fire.

## Rulebook Alignment

Source checked: local Daggerheart PDF, Chapter 5, "Colossus of the Drylands," especially the colossus construction guidance and examples on PDF pages 316-326.

Useful rulebook guidance for this colossus:

- Colossi use multiple adversary stat blocks, each representing a body segment, inside a whole-colossus framework.
- Segments list adjacency, Difficulty, HP, ATK, standard attack, and features.
- The framework holds whole-colossus identity, thresholds, stress, experiences, and features that apply to the entire creature.
- Segments can become Broken. While Broken, a segment cannot use actions or reactions until cleared.
- Destroyed segments stop using their features, but they do not need to physically detach.
- A colossus can be defeated by destroying all segments, but alternate defeat conditions are explicitly supported.
- Puzzle logic is encouraged: protected segments, special damage windows, weak points, range restrictions, collapse triggers, and chain destruction are all appropriate.
- PCs on a segment have advantage on attacks against that segment, and that segment's standard attack generally cannot target them.
- The GM spotlights one segment as a GM move, then can spend Fear to spotlight additional segments. Framework actions can also be spotlighted.
- If an effect targets the whole colossus, use the highest relevant segment Difficulty.
- Additional adversaries should be limited and purposeful.
- The hunt phase should give the party clues about the colossus before the fight.

Glocktopus-specific pattern choices:

- Build it as a Tier 2 Colossus for six level-4 PCs.
- Use a whole-colossus Ammo Cycle to keep the fight readable.
- Use Chain (A) on the weapon tentacles: once enough tentacles are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the mantle loses Protected.
- Use Fatal on the Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage.
- Use Broken and permanent jams to let non-damage solutions matter.
- Use Weak Point on the mantle once the guard opens, rewarding PCs who climb into danger.

## Tuning Target

Draft target: six level-4 PCs.

Level 4 sits in Tier 2 adversary territory. The Glocktopus should be more dangerous than a Tier 1 starter colossus, but it has eight weapon segments, so each individual segment should be simpler than a full solo adversary. The danger comes from combinations, firing lanes, and forced movement.

Pacing target:

- Expected length: about 5-7 meaningful rounds once the fight begins.
- Early fight: two active weapon tentacles per GM move.
- Mid fight: two active weapon tentacles, plus occasional Fear combos.
- Final phase: mantle plus one or two remaining functional weapon tentacles.
- Avoid hitting the same PC with more than two damaging tentacle attacks in one GM move unless that PC deliberately stays exposed in a lethal position.

Recommended starting numbers:

- Framework thresholds: 16 / 25.
- Framework Stress: 6.
- Most weapon tentacles: Difficulty 14-15, HP 3-4, ATK +1 or +2.
- Heavy weapon tentacles: Difficulty 15, HP 5, ATK +1 or +2, more telegraphing.
- Mantle: Difficulty 16, HP 6, ATK +3, Fatal.

If the party has unusually high damage output, add +1 HP to the Gatling Tentacle, Cannon Tentacle, and Mantle. If the party is mostly melee or has poor mobility, reduce the Rifle and Cannon Difficulty by 1 or add more durable cover.

## Visual Design

Overall:

- Giant octopus moving across cracked mud, docks, or a half-flooded settlement.
- Slick blue-black skin with powder burns, brass shell casings embedded in suckers, and old scars shaped like muzzle flashes.
- Eight enormous tentacles, each coiled around or grown through a distinct firearm.
- The head/mantle is domed and glassy like a deep-sea eye, with a beak-cage underneath.
- Ink clouds mix with gunsmoke into an oily black haze.

The guns should look scavenged and impossible rather than modern-clean:

- Revolver cylinder the size of a wagon wheel.
- Shotgun made of bundled church bells and drainpipes.
- Long rifle with a lighthouse lens scope.
- Hand-cranked Gatling assembly fed by belts of ship-chain cartridges.
- Naval cannon strapped to a tentacle with anchor chain.
- Harpoon gun with barbed line and winch.
- Flare mortar that paints targets in hot red signal light.
- Net launcher loaded with weighted cable, hooks, and sticky kelp.

## Encounter Identity

The Glocktopus is about battlefield control through overlapping threat profiles.

Each tentacle asks a different question:

- Revolver: can you handle precise dueling shots?
- Shotgun: can you avoid clustering?
- Rifle: can you break line of sight?
- Gatling: can you stop sustained suppression?
- Cannon: can you survive slow telegraphed devastation?
- Harpoon: can you prevent forced movement and separation?
- Flare Mortar: can you remove marks before the big guns find you?
- Net Launcher: can you keep mobile when the ground becomes a trap?

The GM should not spotlight all eight tentacles at once. The framework chooses a small active firing pattern, then spending Fear can add another tentacle or trigger a combo.

## Framework Stat Block

### The Glocktopus

Tier 2 Colossus

A land-crawling octopus whose eight weaponized tentacles turn the battlefield into a moving firing range.

Motives & Tactics: Ambush, corral, suppress, drag prey, crack cover, punish open ground

Size: about 70 ft. tall when reared up, 140 ft. across when all tentacles are spread

Segments: 8 Weapon Tentacles and 1 Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage

Thresholds: 16 / 25 | Stress: 6

Experience: Huge +2, Gunsmoke +2, Predator Patience +3

Features:

- **Colossal Power - Reaction:** When the Glocktopus fails an attack, gain a Fear.
- **Ammo Cycle - Passive:** At the start of each GM move, choose or roll two functional weapon tentacles. Those tentacles become active until the next GM move. The GM can spotlight one active tentacle as their move, then spend Fear as usual to spotlight additional active tentacles.
- **Bad Roll, Empty Chamber - Passive:** If Ammo Cycle rolls or chooses a Destroyed or permanently jammed tentacle, do not replace it. The Glocktopus has lost that part of its firing rhythm.
- **Reload Window - Passive:** After a weapon tentacle uses a Fear action, it becomes Reloading until the end of the next GM move. While Reloading, the tentacle cannot use attacks, and PCs have advantage on action rolls to climb, damage, jam, or sabotage it.
- **Powder-Ink Haze - Action:** Mark a Stress to flood the battlefield with oily ink and gunsmoke. Until the haze clears, attacks made beyond Close range have disadvantage unless the attacker is targeting a muzzle flash, glowing flare mark, or otherwise clearly revealed segment.
- **Shake Free - Action:** Spend a Fear to throw off riders. All creatures on the Glocktopus must make a Strength or Agility Reaction Roll. Targets who fail fall to the ground or an adjacent lower segment and must mark 1 HP. Targets who succeed must mark 1 Stress or move to an adjacent segment.

## Weapon Jam Rules

A PC can attempt a risky action to jam, bind, misalign, or sabotage a weapon tentacle. Use the target segment's Difficulty. The PC has advantage if they are on that segment or the segment is Reloading.

On a success, choose one:

- The tentacle is Broken until the end of the next GM move.
- If the tentacle was already Broken or Reloading, it becomes permanently jammed.
- The tentacle marks 1 HP and the PC creates a fictional advantage, such as severed ammo, a cracked lens, a fouled barrel, or a tangled line.

A permanently jammed tentacle counts as Destroyed for Crossed Guard and Ammo Cycle, but it does not have to physically detach.

On a success with Fear, the sabotage works, but the tentacle thrashes, moves the PC, destroys nearby cover, or gives another active weapon a clean shot.

## Segment Stat Blocks

### Revolver Tentacle

Adjacent Segments: Mantle, Shotgun Tentacle, Harpoon Tentacle

Difficulty: 14 | HP: 4

ATK: +2 | Quickdraw Snap: Far | 2d8+5 phy

Features:

- **Chain (A) - Passive:** When at least four weapon tentacles in Chain A are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage loses Protected.
- **Fan the Hammer - Action:** Spend a Fear to make a standard attack against up to three targets in the same firing lane. If only one target is exposed, make one attack and add +1d6 damage instead of attacking them repeatedly.
- **Cylinder Jam - Reaction:** When this segment becomes Broken, the next Ammo Cycle cannot choose the Revolver Tentacle.
- **Sabotage Hook:** Jam the cylinder, steal a massive cartridge, or force it to fire into another tentacle.

### Shotgun Tentacle

Adjacent Segments: Mantle, Revolver Tentacle, Net Launcher Tentacle

Difficulty: 14 | HP: 4

ATK: +2 | Saltblast: Very Close | 2d6+8 phy

Features:

- **Chain (A) - Passive:** When at least four weapon tentacles in Chain A are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage loses Protected.
- **Scattershot - Passive:** This segment's standard attack can target a group in front of it within range.
- **Door-Kicker Bloom - Action:** Spend a Fear to make a standard attack with advantage against all targets clustered within Very Close range of one another. On a success, the target is knocked to a worse position or pushed to Close range.
- **Sabotage Hook:** Get inside the barrel angle, wedge debris in the choke, or lure it into blasting cover apart.

### Rifle Tentacle

Adjacent Segments: Mantle, Gatling Tentacle, Flare Mortar Tentacle

Difficulty: 15 | HP: 4

ATK: +2 | Scope-Lens Shot: Very Far | 2d10+4 phy

Features:

- **Chain (A) - Passive:** When at least four weapon tentacles in Chain A are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage loses Protected.
- **Sightline - Passive:** This segment ignores flimsy cover, but not solid cover or broken line of sight.
- **Called Shot - Action:** Spend a Fear to make a standard attack. On a success, the target also drops a held item, loses their footing, or has their next movement complicated by pain.
- **Scope Flare - Reaction:** When a PC uses bright light, reflective cover, or flare paste against this segment and succeeds on an action roll, the Rifle Tentacle becomes Broken.
- **Sabotage Hook:** Shatter the scope, break line of sight, flash the lens, or climb beneath the barrel.

### Gatling Tentacle

Adjacent Segments: Mantle, Rifle Tentacle, Cannon Tentacle

Difficulty: 15 | HP: 5

ATK: +1 | Suppressive Rattle: Far | 2d8+6 phy

Features:

- **Chain (A) - Passive:** When at least four weapon tentacles in Chain A are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage loses Protected.
- **Suppressive Fire - Passive:** On a successful standard attack, the target must move to cover or mark 1 Stress.
- **Brass Rain - Action:** Spend a Fear to choose a lane within Far range. Make a standard attack against each target crossing or standing in that open lane. Until the next GM move, moving through that lane requires an Agility Reaction Roll or the target marks 1 Stress.
- **Overheat - Reaction:** When the Gatling Tentacle marks 2 or more HP from a single attack, it becomes Broken as the barrels glow red and seize.
- **Sabotage Hook:** Cut the ammo belt, overheat the barrels, foul the crank, or use the recoil to swing the tentacle away.

### Cannon Tentacle

Adjacent Segments: Mantle, Gatling Tentacle, Harpoon Tentacle

Difficulty: 15 | HP: 5

ATK: +1 | Warning Boom: Far | 2d12+8 phy

Features:

- **Chain (A) - Passive:** When at least four weapon tentacles in Chain A are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage loses Protected.
- **Slow Aim - Passive:** Before this segment makes a standard attack, clearly telegraph where the barrel is turning. PCs who immediately dive for cover or abandon that position have advantage on any reaction roll against the attack.
- **Broadside - Action:** Spend 2 Fear to make a standard attack against a large area or hard cover. On a success, targets take 2d20+8 physical damage. On a failure, the blast still destroys or reshapes terrain in that area.
- **Backblast - Reaction:** When this segment becomes Broken while aimed at the Mantle, the Mantle marks 1 HP.
- **Sabotage Hook:** Spike the touch-hole, redirect the barrel while it turns, wet the powder, or make it fire before it is aimed.

### Harpoon Tentacle

Adjacent Segments: Mantle, Cannon Tentacle, Revolver Tentacle

Difficulty: 14 | HP: 4

ATK: +2 | Hook and Drag: Far | 2d8+4 phy

Features:

- **Chain (A) - Passive:** When at least four weapon tentacles in Chain A are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage loses Protected.
- **Drag Line - Passive:** On a successful standard attack, pull the target toward the Glocktopus, away from an ally, or onto an adjacent segment.
- **Reel Them In - Action:** Spend a Fear to make a standard attack. On a success, the target is dragged into another active tentacle's ideal range.
- **Anchored Line - Reaction:** When a PC anchors the harpoon line to heavy terrain and succeeds on an action roll, this segment becomes Broken and the Glocktopus cannot move away from that terrain until the next GM move.
- **Sabotage Hook:** Cut the line, jam the winch, ride the harpoon line up, or anchor it to terrain.

### Flare Mortar Tentacle

Adjacent Segments: Mantle, Rifle Tentacle, Net Launcher Tentacle

Difficulty: 14 | HP: 3

ATK: +2 | Marking Flare: Far | 2d6+5 mag or phy

Features:

- **Chain (A) - Passive:** When at least four weapon tentacles in Chain A are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage loses Protected.
- **Signal Paint - Passive:** On a successful standard attack, the target glows with signal light until they spend an action to douse, cover, or scrape it away.
- **Target Painted - Action:** Spend a Fear. A glowing target immediately becomes the preferred target of the next active weapon tentacle attack. If the target is still glowing when hit, add +1d6 damage. If no target is glowing, make a Marking Flare attack first.
- **Hot Paste - Reaction:** When flare paste is scraped onto a weapon tentacle and the PC succeeds on an action roll, the next attack against that tentacle has advantage.
- **Sabotage Hook:** Smother the flare tubes, redirect the signal shell, scrape flare paste onto a tentacle, or use water or ink to douse the mark.

### Net Launcher Tentacle

Adjacent Segments: Mantle, Shotgun Tentacle, Flare Mortar Tentacle

Difficulty: 14 | HP: 3

ATK: +2 | Weighted Net: Far | 2d6+5 phy

Features:

- **Chain (A) - Passive:** When at least four weapon tentacles in Chain A are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage loses Protected.
- **Cable Snare - Passive:** On a successful standard attack, the target's movement is restricted until they cut, burn, or slip free.
- **Trawl the Deck - Action:** Spend a Fear to make a standard attack against targets in a line or cluster. On a success, pull them together or pin them in place for another tentacle's setup.
- **Tangled Arsenal - Reaction:** When this segment is Broken by clever positioning, choose one adjacent weapon tentacle. That tentacle is also Broken until the end of the next GM move.
- **Sabotage Hook:** Cut the net before it spreads, tangle two tentacles together, burn the cable, or let it catch on the environment.

### Glassy Mantle / Beak-Cage

Adjacent Segments: All weapon tentacles

Difficulty: 16 | HP: 6

ATK: +3 | Beak Snap: Very Close | 2d10+8 phy

Features:

- **Fatal - Passive:** When this segment is Destroyed, the Glocktopus is defeated.
- **Protected - Passive:** The Mantle cannot be damaged while five or more weapon tentacles remain functional. Once four weapon tentacles are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Mantle can be damaged by attacks made from the body or within Close range beneath it. Once six weapon tentacles are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Mantle can be damaged from Far range.
- **Weak Point - Passive:** Once Protected is lost, attacks made against the Mantle from Melee range mark 1 additional HP if they mark any HP.
- **Beak-Cage - Passive:** This segment's standard attack can target climbers on the Mantle or PCs directly beneath the body.
- **Eightfold Kill Solution - Action:** Spend 2 Fear to coordinate two functional weapon tentacles in sequence, such as Flare Mortar into Rifle, Net Launcher into Cannon, or Harpoon into Shotgun. Do not include the Cannon Tentacle unless it was already visibly aimed or the party had a clear chance to scatter.

## Encounter Flow

1. The party first sees the Glocktopus from too far away: smoke blooms, then the sound arrives late.
2. The battlefield has meaningful cover, elevation, water or mud, and at least a few objects large enough to survive one hit.
3. The Glocktopus starts with the Mantle protected by crossed tentacles.
4. At the start of each GM move, Ammo Cycle makes two weapon tentacles active. Spotlight one active segment, or spotlight the framework for a whole-colossus action such as Powder-Ink Haze or Shake Free.
5. PCs climb, jam, destroy, redirect, or bait tentacles to open safer approaches.
6. Once four weapon tentacles are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Mantle becomes targetable at close approach.
7. Once six weapon tentacles are Destroyed or permanently jammed, the Mantle is exposed at range.
8. Destroy the Mantle or trigger an alternate defeat condition to end the fight.

Possible alternate defeat conditions:

- Force the Cannon Tentacle to fire into the Mantle after Protected is lost.
- Tangle at least three tentacles in Net Launcher and Harpoon lines, then collapse the Glocktopus long enough for a killing blow.
- Overload the Gatling and Cannon ammunition stores in the same beat, causing the Mantle to mark 3 HP.
- Lure the Glocktopus onto dry land long enough that its skin cracks and the Mantle loses Protected after only three disabled tentacles.

## Level-4 Tuning Notes

If the fight stalls:

- Count a temporarily Broken tentacle as disabled for Crossed Guard until the next GM move.
- Let successful sabotage mark 1 HP on the tentacle in addition to its fiction.
- Expose the Mantle after three disabled tentacles if the table has spent several rounds solving the weapon puzzle but cannot convert it into damage.
- Have a Powder Cult Spotter accidentally reveal the next active tentacle.

If the fight is too easy:

- The Glocktopus activates three weapon tentacles on the first GM move after the Mantle is exposed.
- The Mantle gains +1 HP.
- The Gatling or Cannon clears Broken when the framework marks Stress.
- Powder-Ink Haze lasts until a PC spends an action clearing sight lines with wind, water, light, or a similar effect.

If the fight is too lethal:

- Cannon Broadside costs 3 Fear instead of 2.
- Fan the Hammer cannot target more than two PCs.
- Flare Mortar marks targets but does not add bonus damage until the final phase.
- Destroying or permanently jamming any tentacle immediately clears 1 Stress from the PC who did it.

## Hunt Phase Clues

Use the hunt to teach the weapon puzzle before the fight:

- A fishing village has eight different kinds of impact damage in impossible proximity.
- Survivors argue about what killed their homes because every witness heard a different gun.
- Giant sucker marks are ringed with spent brass.
- A bell tower has been turned inside-out by shotgun spread.
- A lighthouse lens is missing and distant victims have neat, impossible bullet holes.
- Long trenches show where harpooned wagons were dragged toward the water.
- Nets full of hooks hang from rooftops, but the hooks are deep-sea bone.
- Red flare paste still glows on ruined walls, and places marked with it were later obliterated.
- Children play a counting rhyme about when to hide: one click, two clicks, red light, boom.

## Regional Corruption

The Glocktopus changes a settlement into a paranoid firing range:

- People speak in firing lanes and cover positions.
- Streets are renamed after calibers, ranges, and safe angles.
- Every argument starts with someone counting visible exits.
- Local gunsmiths are revered, blamed, and terrified.
- Fishermen refuse to cast nets because nets mean the thing is close.
- Children collect shell casings as charms.
- The richest homes have cannonproof walls; the poorest are made of patched target boards.
- At night, everyone sleeps below window height.

## Followers And Scene Pressure

Keep additional enemies limited and purposeful.

Possible followers:

- Powder Cult Spotters: signal the Glocktopus with lanterns, mirrors, or flare paste.
- Salvage Gunners: desperate locals who worship the shell casings it leaves behind.
- Ink-Drowned Thralls: people pulled under and returned with black eyes and steady trigger hands.
- Brass Crabs: small scavenger constructs or crustacean-like hazards that carry ammunition to tentacles.

Followers should support the weapon puzzle, not become the main encounter.

## Tone Notes

The name is a joke, but the encounter should not be only a joke.

Play the Glocktopus like absurd escalation:

- It is ridiculous that it has eight guns.
- It is scary that each one changes the map.
- It is satisfying when the party turns the guns against each other.

Good table feel:

- Announce the active guns clearly.
- Telegraph the Cannon and Rifle before they fire.
- Let clever sabotage matter even when it does not immediately destroy a segment.
- Use cover visibly and generously, then let the Glocktopus destroy some of it.
- Avoid eight separate enemy turns. The framework should make the monster feel huge without making the round feel slow.
